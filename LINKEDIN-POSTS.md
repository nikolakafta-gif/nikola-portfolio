## Post 3: Honest pricing talk

"How much does a website cost?"

I get this question every week. Here is the honest answer:

A simple one-page site: $800 to $2,000
A 3-5 page business website: $1,500 to $3,500
Something more complex with animations and CMS: $3,000 to $5,000

What should always be included in that price:
- Custom design (not a template)
- Mobile responsive
- SEO setup so Google can find you
- Fast load speed

What are red flags in a quote:
- No breakdown of what you are paying for
- Price under $300 (you will pay twice when it breaks)
- "Extras" for mobile or SEO (that is not extra, that is standard)

I wrote a full pricing guide on my blog if you want the details: nikolamiljkovic.live/blog/web-developer-cost

## Post 4: Template vs custom

I see this all the time:

A business owner buys a $49 Wix template. Spends a weekend dragging boxes around. Launches something that looks like every other site in their industry.

6 months later, they realize:
- Google is not sending any traffic
- The site is slow on phones
- They can not change the layout without breaking everything
- Their competitor's custom site looks 10x more professional

Then they come to me and we build it properly.

The template did not save them money. It cost them 6 months of lost credibility and zero Google traffic.

Templates work for personal blogs and hobby projects. If your website is supposed to bring you clients, invest in something built for your business specifically.
