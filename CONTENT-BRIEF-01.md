## FAQ Section (8 Questions)

1. **How much does a one-page website cost?**
   A one-page website or landing page typically costs between $1,500 and $3,000 from a freelance developer. The price depends on design complexity, animations, and whether you need a contact form or CMS integration.

2. **How much does a small business website cost?**
   A standard small business website with 3 to 5 pages costs between $3,000 and $6,000. This includes custom design, mobile responsiveness, basic SEO setup, and a contact form. More complex features like booking systems or e-commerce add to the cost.

3. **Do freelance web developers charge hourly or fixed price?**
   Most freelancers offer fixed-price quotes for complete projects, which gives you cost certainty. Hourly rates typically range from $50 to $150 per hour, but fixed pricing is more common because clients prefer knowing the total cost upfront.

4. **Why are some web developers so much cheaper than others?**
   Cheaper developers often use templates, skip mobile optimization, ignore SEO, and deliver code that breaks when you need to make changes. The upfront savings usually cost more long-term through redesigns, lost traffic, and maintenance issues.

5. **What is the average cost of website maintenance?**
   Ongoing website maintenance typically costs between $50 and $200 per month. This covers security updates, performance monitoring, content updates, and SEO upkeep. Without maintenance, a website gradually becomes outdated and vulnerable.

6. **Should I pay for SEO separately or is it included?**
   Basic SEO (meta tags, sitemap, structured data, page speed optimization) should be included in every web development project. Advanced SEO like keyword research, content strategy, and link building is a separate ongoing service.

7. **How much should I budget for a website redesign?**
   A website redesign costs about the same as building a new site, between $3,000 and $8,000 for a standard business website. The main additional cost is content migration and setting up proper redirects to protect your existing search rankings.

8. **Is a custom website worth the extra cost over a template?**
   Yes, for businesses that rely on their website for credibility and lead generation. A custom website loads faster, ranks better on Google, looks unique, and can be tailored to convert your specific audience. Templates save money upfront but limit your growth.
